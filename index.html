About TechBridge

Coding explained in a way beginners can actually use.

TechBridge is a nonprofit created to help younger students learn coding in a simple and supportive environment. Many students think coding is confusing or only for advanced learners, so TechBridge breaks it down into easier steps.

The program teaches students problem solving, creativity, and confidence with technology through clear lessons, guided practice, and projects that make each concept feel real.

Clear lessons

Ideas are introduced step by step so students can follow along without feeling rushed.

Hands-on practice

Students learn by writing code, testing ideas, fixing errors, and trying again.

Supportive teaching

The tone stays friendly and beginner-focused, with room for questions and mistakes.

Our First Course

An 8-week coding program with real students.

The curriculum had Google Drive slides labeled Week 1 through Week 5, but the full course continued for 8 weeks total. The last weeks were used for extra practice, projects, review, and applying what students learned.

Week 1

Introduction to coding

Students learned what coding is and explored basic computer science ideas.

Week 2

Programming concepts

The class practiced simple commands and learned how instructions become programs.

Week 3

Variables and logic

Students used variables, logic, and problem solving to organize their code.

Week 4

Loops and conditions

The course introduced repeated actions, decisions, and building small programs.

Week 5

Guided activities

Students got more practice with coding skills through structured examples.

Week 6

Project planning

Students chose project ideas and planned how to use their new coding skills.

Week 7

Project building

The class built, tested, and improved student projects with support.

Week 8

Final work and sharing

Students reviewed key ideas, finished projects, and shared what they created.

What Students Learned

Beginner skills, stronger confidence, and practice that sticks.

Students learned beginner coding skills, problem solving, and how to think through errors. The course focused on learning by doing instead of only listening to lectures.

Coding basics

Students practiced commands, structure, and the foundations of writing simple programs.

Problem solving

Lessons encouraged students to break big tasks into smaller, easier steps.

Logic and creativity

Students used logic to make decisions while also creating their own project ideas.

Hands-on projects

Practice activities became small projects students could test, change, and share.

Confidence with technology

The course helped students see technology as something they can understand.

Teamwork and communication

Students asked questions, explained their thinking, and shared what they built.
